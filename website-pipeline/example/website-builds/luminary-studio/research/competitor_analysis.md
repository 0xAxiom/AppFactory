## Detailed Analysis

### 1. Pentagram

**Website:** pentagram.com

**Strengths:**
- Legendary reputation, 50+ years
- Partner-led structure attracts top talent
- Massive, impressive portfolio
- Editorial-quality case studies

**Weaknesses:**
- Website feels dated (minimal interactivity)
- Pricing out of reach for startups
- Long timelines (6+ months)
- Intimidating for smaller clients

**Design Observations:**
- Minimal, black and white
- Grid-based portfolio
- Long-form case studies
- No animations

---

### 2. Collins

**Website:** wearecollins.com

**Strengths:**
- Modern, bold aesthetic
- Strong motion design
- Cultural relevance (Spotify, Dropbox, Twitch)
- Excellent case study storytelling

**Weaknesses:**
- Exclusive feel may deter smaller clients
- Limited services information
- No clear path to inquiry
- Portfolio-heavy, light on services

**Design Observations:**
- Full-bleed imagery
- Experimental typography
- Smooth transitions
- Video-heavy case studies

---

### 3. Ramotion

**Website:** ramotion.com

**Strengths:**
- Clear startup focus
- Technical credibility (UI/UX + branding)
- Transparent about capabilities
- Active blog/resources

**Weaknesses:**
- Website feels busy
- Case studies lack depth
- Generic tech aesthetic
- Cluttered navigation

**Design Observations:**
- Gradient accents
- Card-based layouts
- Hover animations
- Blog-forward content strategy

---

### 4. Focus Lab

**Website:** focuslab.agency

**Strengths:**
- Clear B2B SaaS positioning
- Process transparency
- Excellent case study depth
- Strong thought leadership

**Weaknesses:**
- Narrow focus limits appeal
- Conservative design choices
- Limited interactivity
- Text-heavy pages

**Design Observations:**
- Clean, corporate aesthetic
- Process diagrams
- Before/after comparisons
- Testimonial integration

---

### 5. Unfold

**Website:** unfold.co

**Strengths:**
- Startup-friendly positioning
- Modern, minimal website
- Quick inquiry process
- International appeal

**Weaknesses:**
- Smaller portfolio
- Less brand recognition
- Limited team visibility
- Services unclear

**Design Observations:**
- Whitespace-forward
- Subtle animations
- Mobile-optimized
- Dark mode support

## Competitive Gaps & Opportunities

### Gap 1: Interactive Experience
Most competitor sites are static portfolios. Opportunity for Luminary to create memorable, interactive experience that demonstrates design craft.

### Gap 2: Process Transparency
Few competitors explain their process clearly. Luminary can differentiate by showing exactly how they work.

### Gap 3: Approachable Premium
Pentagram/Collins are premium but intimidating. Ramotion/Unfold are approachable but less impressive. Opportunity for premium quality with approachable positioning.

### Gap 4: Speed Positioning
None explicitly position around speed. Luminary can capture startup market by promising faster delivery.

### Gap 5: Technical Credibility
Most brand agencies feel separate from tech world. Luminary can appeal to tech founders by demonstrating technical literacy.
